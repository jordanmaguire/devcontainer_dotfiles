# Dictate

The user has provided dictated or stream-of-consciousness input. Your job is to read through the messy thoughts and produce a useful summary.

## How to respond

1. Read all the way through the input before responding — early sentences often get contradicted, refined, or abandoned later. Dictation is not a linear argument.
2. Identify the user's core intent: what are they actually trying to figure out, decide, or communicate?
3. Produce a structured response with these sections:
   - **Summary** — 2–3 sentences capturing the gist
   - **Key points** — bullets for the distinct ideas, observations, or constraints
   - **Action items or open questions** — anything that needs a decision or follow-up, even if only implied

## Guidelines

- Strip verbal artifacts (um, uh, like, you know, sort of, kind of, I mean) and false starts.
- Preserve the user's actual voice and terminology. Don't paraphrase into corporate-speak or flatten distinctive word choices.
- If the user contradicts themselves, surface the contradiction rather than silently picking one side.
- If a passage is genuinely ambiguous and the rest of the input doesn't disambiguate it, ask rather than fabricate clarity.
- Keep the response shorter than the input. A summary longer than what was dictated has failed.
- Don't add advice, opinions, or next steps the user didn't ask for. The job is to clarify their thinking, not replace it.
